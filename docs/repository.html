Contribute to the Archive Submit a Badman

The Detroit Badman Archive grows through community contributions. If you know of a figure—real or fictional—who embodies the badman tradition in Detroit, we want to hear from you.

Your submission serves as a research lead. Our team will conduct independent verification and in-depth research before adding approved figures to the archive. This process ensures scholarly rigor while honoring community knowledge.

What happens after you submit: You'll receive an email confirming whether your submission has been approved for research. Once the figure is live in the archive, you'll receive a second notification with a direct link to the entry.

Evaluation Framework The Five Criteria

Submissions are evaluated against five criteria that define the badman tradition:

1. Outlaw Relationship to Law: Does the figure operate outside or against legal systems?

2. Community Authorization: Does the figure have recognition and support from Black communities?

3. Violence as Language: Does the figure use force as a mode of expression or resistance?

4. Cultural Preservation Function: Does the figure play a role in maintaining Black cultural knowledge?

5. Hypermasculine Performance: Does the figure embody Black masculine heroic ideals?

Ready to Contribute? Submission Form

Click the button below to access the submission form. You'll be asked to provide:

  • Your name and email (for notification purposes only—never displayed publicly)
  • Your city (limited to metropolitan area for privacy)
  • The badman's name
  • Whether the figure is real or fictional
  • How you encountered this badman (book, film, family story, etc.)
  • Any sources or documentation you can provide

Biography

Jericho 'Action' Jackson is a Detroit Police Department sergeant who combines Ivy League education with street-level brutality. A Harvard Law graduate, Jackson was demoted from lieutenant after nearly tearing the arm off of Sean Dellaplane, son of powerful auto industry magnate Peter Dellaplane. When Dellaplane Sr. begins systematically murdering union officials to consolidate political power, Jackson investigates—only to be framed for the murder of Dellaplane's wife. Going vigilante with the help of Sydney Ash, a nightclub singer Dellaplane has addicted to heroin, Jackson wages a one-man war against the industrial conspiracy. The character emerged from Carl Weathers's conversations with producer Joel Silver about their shared love of 1970s blaxploitation films, updating that tradition for Reagan-era action cinema.

Outlaw Relationship to Law 4 / 5
Operates within legal system as Detroit police sergeant but was demoted for excessive force. Goes fully vigilante after being framed for murder, operating outside law to clear his name and bring down Dellaplane.
Community Authorization 4 / 5
Protects auto workers union members against corporate assassination. Partners with Sydney Ash, representing protection of vulnerable community members. However, his authorization comes more from institutional badge than grassroots community.
Violence as Language 5 / 5
Violence defines his character from opening scene. Demoted for nearly tearing off a man's arm. The film is structured around escalating physical confrontations, car chases, explosions, and hand-to-hand combat.
Cultural Preservation Function 3 / 5
Preserves and updates blaxploitation tradition for 1980s audiences. Carl Weathers explicitly conceived the character from his love of 1970s blaxploitation films. However, the film prioritizes action spectacle over cultural commentary.
Hypermasculine Performance 5 / 5
Peak hypermasculinity: Harvard-educated but physically dominant, witty but brutal. Carl Weathers's physique is central to the character. Reputation precedes him—criminals faint at the mention of his name.

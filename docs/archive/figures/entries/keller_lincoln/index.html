Biography

Lincoln 'Linc' Keller is a Black private investigator operating in Detroit, created by native Detroiter Dr. Lee E. Meadows. A former Oakland Raiders defensive back who became a police officer in Oakland before returning to his hometown, Keller runs a PI practice navigating Detroit's layered criminal and political landscape. He is embedded in a family network: brothers Jefferson (bounty hunter), Truman, and Roosevelt, and sister Julie 'the Duchess of the Database' who runs the computer research firm Info-Search across the hall from his office. His romantic interest is Candy Malone, a Detroit homicide detective. In Silent Conspiracy (1997), Keller investigates the forty-year disappearance of a 1950s R&B group called The Sentiments, uncovering Detroit's hidden music history. In Silent Suspicion (2000), he reopens the cold-case murder of freelance journalist Deborah Norris, discovering corruption linking an aspiring mayoral candidate to bribes and cover-ups. Keller works the Cass Corridor and Detroit's inner city with methodical intelligence, reading the city's social grammar to protect those the official system has failed.

Five-Criteria Evaluation

Each figure is scored against the five criteria that define the badman tradition. Scores range 1–5.

Outlaw Relationship to Law 3 / 5
Former Oakland police officer who left the institution to work independently as a private investigator in Detroit. Not anti-law but outside its authority. PI license gives him liminal status — authorized but not controlled. Regularly clashes with Lt. Knackton, who represents institutional resistance to independent Black investigation.
Community Authorization 3 / 5
Family-embedded — the Keller siblings (all named after US presidents) form a support network. Connected to Detroit's music community through the first novel's Motown-era mystery. Romantic interest Candy Malone bridges Keller to DPD homicide. Three-time AALBC bestseller indicates meaningful authorization within the Black literary community, though very limited readership overall.
Violence as Language 5 / 5
Former NFL defensive back (Oakland Raiders) and former police officer — trained for physical violent confrontation at the highest levels. While Keller's investigative style is described as cerebral and methodical, he is fully capable of and experienced in physical force. The detective's restraint is strategic, not temperamental — a man who can hit like a professional athlete but reads the room instead is communicating through his restraint.
Cultural Preservation Function 4 / 5
The novels preserve 1990s Detroit street culture and 1950s Black music history with what reviewers call unmatched authenticity. Silent Conspiracy reconstructs Detroit's pre-Motown R&B scene. Author Meadows's WPON radio show The Book Beat extended this preservation function by interviewing African American authors weekly. The novels document a Detroit that no longer exists.
Hypermasculine Performance 3 / 5
Former NFL defensive back — the physical credential is present. But the character's masculinity is expressed through wit, intelligence, family loyalty, and cynical humor rather than physical dominance. Described as gentle and caring alongside his toughness.

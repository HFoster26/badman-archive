Biography

Kenyatta is the protagonist of Donald Goines's four-novel series published under the pseudonym Al C. Clark. Named after Jomo Kenyatta, the father of Kenyan independence, he leads a militant organization of Black men and women dedicated to cleansing Detroit's ghettos of drug dealers and racist police officers. Operating from a fortified farm outside the city where he trains his soldiers, Kenyatta wages a systematic war against the forces destroying his community. The series follows his escalating conflict with both the criminal underworld and law enforcement, culminating in a cross-country chase that ends in Las Vegas. Kenyatta represents Goines's vision of what organized Black resistance might look like—a disciplined, armed, community-authorized force operating outside the law to protect those the law refuses to serve.

Timeline

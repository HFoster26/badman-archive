Events

The archive hosts community collection events in partnership with Detroit cultural institutions — digitization days, oral history sessions, and community intake gatherings where residents can contribute stories, photographs, and family memories to the archive in person.

About the events program

The archive's events program exists because some of the most important material in the tradition doesn't live online. It lives in family photo albums, in oral memory, in the shoebox of clippings an elder has kept for fifty years. Events bring the archive to the people who hold this material so that contributing is easy, safe, and conducted in trusted community spaces — not through a web form you have to fill out alone.

Every event is staffed by the archive team and hosted at partner institution facilities. Equipment for digitization, audio recording, and document handling is provided. Contributors leave with their original materials in hand — the archive keeps digital copies only.

Partner institutions

The archive is in active outreach with three Detroit cultural institutions whose missions align with the archive's community-benefit commitments. Confirmed partnerships will be announced here as agreements are finalized.

  • The Charles H. Wright Museum of African American History — Detroit's flagship institution for Black history and culture.
  • The Walter P. Reuther Library of Labor and Urban Affairs — Wayne State University's archive of Detroit labor, political, and civil rights history.
  • The E. Azalia Hackley Collection of African Americans in the Performing Arts — the Detroit Public Library's specialized Black performing arts archive.

What to expect

A typical event runs three to four hours at a partner facility. You can drop in for a short contribution or stay for a longer session — both are welcome. Bring whatever you want to contribute: photographs, documents, audio recordings, or just your own story told out loud.

At the event, you'll be greeted by a member of the archive team who will walk you through the submission process, explain the access-level options, and help you decide what to include. If you're contributing documents or photographs, we'll scan them on the spot and return the originals to you immediately. If you're contributing an oral history, we'll record it in a quiet space with professional equipment and send you a copy of the recording.

You can contribute anonymously, under your own name, or somewhere in between. The archive respects whatever level of attribution you want for your contribution.

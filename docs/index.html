Detroit Badman Digital Archive

A digital humanities archive documenting Black masculine heroism in Detroit — the detectives, revolutionaries, and superhero-villains whose stories shape the city's cultural memory. Built at Michigan State University's Cultural Heritage Informatics Fellowship as the proof-of-concept for a national multi-city archive.

Browse the figures

What it does Six data buckets per figure

Every figure in the archive is documented through six structured categories: identity, a five-criteria badman score, biography, geography, network connections, and primary sources. The result is a view of Detroit's cultural memory that traditional institutional archives miss — the layer of narrative and reputation that lives in stories, not records.

About A scholarly project, a community record

The Badman Digital Archive treats community members as sources of knowledge rather than subjects of study. Detroit is the proof-of-concept for a national framework that will eventually cover Atlanta, New Orleans, Washington D.C., Memphis, Chicago, and beyond.
